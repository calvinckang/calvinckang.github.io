Dow Jones Risk & Compliance

Risk Profile Page Redesign and Design System

Redesigning a data-dense, financial crime compliance product to enhance its usability and accessibility, and building its design system.

Role: UI Designer
Team: UX Designer, Accessibility Expert, Design System Advocate
Duration: July 2023 - present

Background

RiskCenter Financial Crime is Dow Jones' B2B product under Risk & Compliance, designed for conducting due diligence on potential customers, ongoing monitoring, and sanctions screening. The users of this product include financial institutions looking to ensure compliance with regulations. One of the tasks they often do is performing a database search for risk profiles on entities and individuals, assessing them to make informed decisions.

Problem

From the user feedback received, we identified several problems. Specific to the risk profile page, the users complained that there's a lot of blank space in the UI. Users wanted to view more data at first glance without having to scroll too much, stating that information density is important for them to be more efficient in their workflow. Users also reported a lack of clear visual hierarchy and confusing reading pattern in some sections.

Apart from user feedback, we also found accessibility issues, e.g. unmet minimum color contrast, and missing aria-label and role. The UI also felt outdated and it wasn't built using a design system.

Scope

These are the things we wanted to do in this project:

  • Optimize information density
  • Improve information architecture and visual hierarchy
  • Comply with accessibility
  • Modernize UI
  • Build design system

The goals were to enhance usability, improve user satisfaction, and ultimately, increase sales and retention.

Design Competitive Analysis

My role was UI Designer in this project, collaborating closely and working in parallel with the UX Designer. I started my process by doing a design competitive analysis of the top 10 competitors, among them were Factset and Refinitiv, which both provided a highly optimized screen real estate with information density while maintaining a solid information architecture, clear visual hierarchy, and accessibility compliance.

UI Audit

After an extensive audit of the colors, shadows, typography, icons, and grids, plenty of inconsistencies were identified in the UI, predominantly in the colors. We decided to consolidate redundant colors for greater consistency, and make adjustments to ensure all elements have sufficient color contrast.

Design Direction

The findings from design competitive analysis and UI audit served to inspire and guide the design direction. The chosen concept of our moodboard was Expert Experience, an experience that conveys a sense of expertise and authority, described by these three words: Credible, Authoritative, and Functional.

Visual Exploration

The old left navigation had a clunky look and feel, and some of the icons weren't accurate representations of the items. I explored the possibilities of the icons to convey the true meaning, while ensuring the style consistency. Some improvements were also made in the visual hierarchy.

On the subject of the page design, several options were made and iterated to achieve what we had in mind in the design direction.

Prototype and User Testing

The chosen design was then turned into an interactive prototype to be tested with user groups in different regions to obtain feedback.

Design System

The design system work was initiated by building the foundations, such as colors, typography, elevation, grids, iconography, etc. Based on the color audit that I did previously, I created the core palettes for this product (primary, secondary, tertiary, and so on), and assigned them to the contextual colors (ink, interface, and interactive). Once the foundations were defined, it was time to build the component library. It's important to keep accessibility in mind throughout this process.

All these assets were handed off to the Developers with thorough documentation. After they built the components in Storybook, I did a QA to ensure alignment between design and development.
